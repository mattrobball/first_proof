## Reviewer Issues

1. **[minor]** (Correctness & Completeness) Global setup / Step 1 and Step 6: The argument implicitly assumes a loopless undirected graph (possibly with parallel edges). Statements like L_e = (e_u-e_v)(e_u-e_v)^T and L_{\{v\}} = 0 are not automatically valid under conventions allowing self-loops (or other Laplacian variants).
2. **[major]** (Clarity & Rigor) Lemma 6.1 proof, barrier bound derivation: The inequality (I - K)^{-1} ≼ (1 - tr K)^{-1} I for PSD K with tr K < 1 is used without proof or citation. This is a non-trivial spectral bound that requires justification.
3. **[major]** (Clarity & Rigor) Lemma 6.1 proof, 'short rearrangement': The algebraic equivalence between condition (34) and the final potential bound Φ_{u'}(M+B) ≤ Φ_u(M) is asserted without demonstration. This is the key step connecting the barrier condition to the potential decrease.
4. **[minor]** (Clarity & Rigor) Step 4, summation identity before equation (41): The claim that Σ_{v,γ} B_v^γ is a 'sub-sum' of {A_e} glosses over verifying that each edge contributes at most once to the double sum over (v, γ).
5. **[minor]** (Clarity & Rigor) Step 1, passage to all of R^V: The phrase 'by symmetry no mixed terms occur' is imprecise. The relevant fact is that ker L ⊆ ker L_S, which ensures the orthogonal decomposition R^V = ker L ⊕ range(L) block-diagonalizes both quadratic forms.
6. **[minor]** (Clarity & Rigor) Steps 1, 3, 4 — notation: The variable u is used both as a vertex label (e = {u,v}, and in the sum in (38)) and as the barrier parameter u = u_t in Step 4. This creates ambiguity, especially in Step 4 where both meanings coexist.
7. **[minor]** (Clarity & Rigor) Step 6, bound r ≤ 32/ε: The step ⌈16/ε⌉ ≤ 16/ε + 1 ≤ 32/ε silently uses ε ≤ 1 ≤ 16/ε. While correct and elementary, it should be noted for completeness.
