## Summary

The solution successfully outlines a correct construction using a spin-lift of a hyperbolic lattice and rational surgery. The central insight of using an idempotent to manage the group ring algebra is effective. However, the proof contains non-trivial gaps in rigor, particularly regarding the explicit vanishing of the surgery obstruction in the orthogonal component and the verification of involution compatibility. There is also a technical error in the citation of stable homotopy groups. The result is correct, but the proof requires expert revision to be fully rigorous.

## Reviewer Issues

1. **[major]** (Correctness & Completeness) Step 5 (Section 7.0.6): The argument that the (1-e) component of the surgery obstruction vanishes is incomplete. The proof claims that elementary contractible summands represent zero in L_n^h, but does not explicitly show that C*(X̃;Q)·(1-e) is contractible or that its symmetric structure is cobordant to zero. This is the most critical logical step — the vanishing of the surgery obstruction — and the decomposition across the idempotent splitting requires more care.
2. **[major]** (Correctness & Completeness) Lemma 7.4: The proof that the involution on eQΓe matches the standard involution on QL under the isomorphism λ ↦ es(λ) is asserted but not verified. The claim 'the involutions agree because e* = e' is insufficient — one must also check that (es(λ))* = es(λ⁻¹), which involves showing that s(λ)⁻¹ and s(λ⁻¹) differ by a power of z that is absorbed by e.
3. **[minor]** (Correctness & Completeness) Step 1 (Section 7.0.2): The claim that Spin(n,1) is a real semisimple group is used without justification. While true (a finite central extension of a semisimple group is semisimple), this is a hypothesis of the problem and should be verified.
4. **[minor]** (Correctness & Completeness) Step 3, equation (45): The Wall finiteness obstruction is stated to vanish 'already in K₀(QΓ), hence also in K̃₀(QΓ),' but the reasoning could be confused. The point is that χ(N) = 0 makes the entire expression zero, regardless of the class of [eQΓ] in K̃₀. The phrase 'already in K₀' might suggest vanishing of [eQΓ] itself.
5. **[minor]** (Correctness & Completeness) Lemma 7.3, H₀ action computation: The computation writes '[1 ⊗ e]γ = [1 ⊗ e·s(μ)z^ε] = [1 ⊗ e·s(μ)]' using ez^ε = e, but then '[1 ⊗ es(μ)] = [1·μ ⊗ e]' uses the tensor product over QL to move s(μ) across. This step implicitly identifies es(μ) with μ·e in the QL-bimodule structure, which requires the reader to recall the isomorphism from Lemma 7.2. A forward reference or brief reminder would help.
6. **[minor]** (Correctness & Completeness) Step 4, Lemma 7.8: The lemma uses the fact that π_i(BSG) is finite for all i > 0 (Serre's theorem on finite stable homotopy groups of spheres) to conclude BSG_{(0)} is contractible. This requires BSG to be simply connected (or at least nilpotent). BSG is in fact simply connected since π₁(BSG) = π₀^S = Z/2, which is finite, so BSG is rationally simply connected. The rationalization argument is standard but the nilpotence hypothesis should be noted.
7. **[major]** (Clarity & Rigor) Lemma 7.8 (paragraph on BSG homotopy groups): The claim "for i>0, π_i(BSG)=π_{i-1}^S" is not correct as written for i=1, and the finiteness conclusion is then not justified from that formula.

9. **[major]** (Clarity & Rigor) Proposition 7.9 (chain equivalence (51)): The step "comparison theorem shows (51) is a chain-homotopy equivalence" is too compressed: augmentation compatibility and the induced map on H_0 are not explicitly verified.
10. **[major]** (Clarity & Rigor) Step 5 (decomposition of surgery obstruction): The claim that the obstruction lies entirely in the e-summand is plausible but not fully spelled out at the level of L-groups and normal complexes.
