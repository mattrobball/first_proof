\[
\int_E F(u+\psi)\,\mu_N(du)=\int_E F(u)\,R_{N,\psi}(u)\,\mu_N(du),\qquad F\in L^\infty(E),

\int_E F(u+\psi)\,\mu(du)=\int_E F(u)\,R_\psi(u)\,\mu(du)
\]
for all bounded continuous \(F\), then for all bounded measurable \(F\) by measure-determining extension on Polish \(E\). Therefore
\[
T_{\psi*}\mu(d u)=R_\psi(u)\,\mu(d u),\qquad T_{\psi*}\mu\ll\mu.

Applying the same construction to \(-\psi\) (Lemma 6), we get \(T_{-\psi*}\mu\ll\mu\). Pushing this forward by \(T_\psi\),
\[
\mu=T_{\psi*}(T_{-\psi*}\mu)\ll T_{\psi*}\mu.

Thus \(\mu\ll T_{\psi*}\mu\) and \(T_{\psi*}\mu\ll\mu\), i.e.

T_{\psi*}\mu\sim\mu.

This is the target theorem.

## Lemma Proofs
Let \(K:=m^2-\Delta\).

### Lemma 1 (Cutoff quasi-invariance)
**Claim.** For each \(N\), \(T_{\psi*}\mu_N\ll\mu_N\), with
\[
R_{N,\psi}(u):=\frac{d\,T_{\psi*}\mu_N}{d\mu_N}(u)
=\exp\!\Big(\langle K\psi,u\rangle-\tfrac12\|\psi\|_{H_m^1}^2+V_N(u)-V_N(u-\psi)\Big)>0.
\]

**Proof.**  
Use \(\mu_N=Z_N^{-1}e^{-V_N}\gamma\) and Cameron–Martin for \(\gamma\):
\[
\frac{d\,T_{\psi*}\gamma}{d\gamma}(u)=\exp\!\Big(\langle K\psi,u\rangle-\tfrac12\|\psi\|_{H_m^1}^2\Big).
\]
Then
\[
T_{\psi*}\mu_N(du)=Z_N^{-1}e^{-V_N(u-\psi)}T_{\psi*}\gamma(du)
=R_{N,\psi}(u)\,\mu_N(du).
\]
Strict positivity is immediate from exponential form. \(\square\)

### Lemma 2 (Renormalized shift expansion)
**Claim.** Writing \(\psi_N=P_N\psi\), \(\phi_N=P_Nu\),
\[
\log R_{N,\psi}=c_{N,\psi}
+\langle K\psi,u\rangle
+\lambda\!\int \psi_N:\phi_N^3:_{C_N}
-\frac{3\lambda}{2}\!\int \psi_N^2:\phi_N^2:_{C_N}
+\lambda\!\int \psi_N^3\phi_N
+\delta m_N\!\int \psi_N\phi_N,
\]
with deterministic
\[
c_{N,\psi}=-\frac12\|\psi\|_{H_m^1}^2-\frac\lambda4\int \psi_N^4
-\frac{\delta m_N}{2}\int \psi_N^2.
\]

**Proof.**  
From Lemma 1, \(\log R_{N,\psi}=\langle K\psi,u\rangle-\frac12\|\psi\|_{H_m^1}^2+V_N(u)-V_N(u-\psi)\).  
Now \(P_N(u-\psi)=\phi_N-\psi_N\), and Wick binomial gives
\[
:(\phi_N-\psi_N)^4:_{C_N}
=:\phi_N^4:_{C_N}
-4\psi_N:\phi_N^3:_{C_N}
+6\psi_N^2:\phi_N^2:_{C_N}
-4\psi_N^3\phi_N+\psi_N^4.
\]
Also
\[
\phi_N^2-(\phi_N-\psi_N)^2=2\psi_N\phi_N-\psi_N^2.
\]
Substitute into \(V_N(u)-V_N(u-\psi)\) to obtain the formula. If one rewrites \(:\phi_N^2:_{C_N}=\phi_N^2-C_N\), the \(C_N\)-terms are deterministic and absorbed into \(c_{N,\psi}\), which is the required counterterm bookkeeping. \(\square\)

### Lemma 3 (Uniform exponential bounds)
**Claim.** There exist \(p,q>1\) such that
\[
\sup_N \mathbb E_{\mu_N}[R_{N,\psi}^{\,p}]<\infty,\qquad
\sup_N \mathbb E_{\mu_N}[R_{N,\psi}^{-q}]<\infty.
\]

**Proof.**  
By Lemma 2, \(\log R_{N,\psi}\) is a finite linear combination of shifted renormalized cubic/quadratic/linear functionals plus deterministic constants. Assumption 4 states uniform exponential integrability strong enough to control exactly these shifted polynomial functionals (and their signs) uniformly in \(N\). Applying Hölder to combine finitely many terms yields the displayed \(p,q>1\) bounds. \(\square\)

### Lemma 4 (Convergence of shifted Wick functionals)
**Claim.** The random terms in \(\log R_{N,\psi}\) converge (in probability, and in \(L^1\) after UI extraction) to corresponding limit renormalized functionals.

**Proof.**  
Assumption 5 gives convergence of Wick powers and shifted Wick powers under the same topology/construction used for \(\mu_N\Rightarrow\mu\). Since \(\psi_N\to\psi\) smoothly and pairings with smooth coefficients are continuous, each term in Lemma 2 converges in probability along the \(\mu_N\)-scheme.  
Uniform exponential moment control from Lemma 3 implies uniform integrability of these terms (and exponentials of their linear combinations), so Vitali upgrades probability convergence to \(L^1\) where needed. \(\square\)

### Lemma 5 (Limit RN density)
**Claim.** There is \(R_\psi>0\) \(\mu\)-a.s., \(R_\psi\in L^1(\mu)\), \(\int R_\psi d\mu=1\), and
\[
\int F(u+\psi)\,d\mu=\int F(u)\,R_\psi(u)\,d\mu
\]
for bounded measurable \(F\).

**Proof.**  
Take any subsequence \(N_k\). By Skorokhod representation for \(\mu_{N_k}\Rightarrow\mu\), realize \(U_k\sim\mu_{N_k}\), \(U\sim\mu\), with \(U_k\to U\) a.s.  
Let \(L_k:=\log R_{N_k,\psi}(U_k)\). By Lemma 4, \(L_k\to L\) in probability; by Lemma 3, \(e^{\pm L_k}\) are UI. Hence \(e^{L_k}\to e^L\) in \(L^1\). Define \(R_\psi(U):=e^L\), giving \(R_\psi>0\) and \(R_\psi\in L^1\). Also
\[
\mathbb E[R_\psi]=\lim_k \mathbb E[e^{L_k}]
=\lim_k \int R_{N_k,\psi}\,d\mu_{N_k}=1.
\]
For bounded continuous \(F\),
\[
\mathbb E[F(U_k+\psi)]
=\mathbb E[F(U_k)e^{L_k}]
\]
(by finite-\(N\) identity from Lemma 1).  
Left side \(\to \mathbb E[F(U+\psi)]\) by \(U_k\to U\), Assumption 6, bounded convergence.  
Right side \(\to \mathbb E[F(U)R_\psi(U)]\) by \(L^1\)-convergence of \(e^{L_k}\) and bounded continuity of \(F\). So
\[
\int F(u+\psi)\,d\mu=\int F(u)R_\psi(u)\,d\mu.
\]
By uniqueness of finite Borel measures determined by bounded continuous test functions on Polish spaces, this extends to all bounded measurable \(F\). Since subsequence was arbitrary, the full sequence has the same limit density. \(\square\)

### Lemma 6 (Dual shift and equivalence)
**Claim.** Repeating for \(-\psi\) yields mutual absolute continuity: \(T_{\psi*}\mu\sim\mu\).

**Proof.**  
Apply Lemmas 1–5 with \(-\psi\): \(T_{-\psi*}\mu\ll\mu\).  
From Lemma 5 for \(+\psi\): \(T_{\psi*}\mu\ll\mu\).  
Pushforward preserves \(\ll\): from \(T_{-\psi*}\mu\ll\mu\), applying \(T_{\psi*}\),
\[
\mu=T_{\psi*}(T_{-\psi*}\mu)\ll T_{\psi*}\mu.
\]
Hence both directions hold, so \(T_{\psi*}\mu\sim\mu\). \(\square\)
