**Lemma 1 (Admissible subgroup closure).**  
If \(H\to G\) is \(\mathcal O\)-admissible and \(J\le H\), then \(J\to G\) is \(\mathcal O\)-admissible.

**Proof.** \(H\to G\) means \(G/H\) is admissible as a \(G\)-set. There is a \(G\)-map \(p:G/J\to G/H\). Indexing systems are pullback/truncation closed, so pullback of an admissible \(G\)-set along any \(G\)-map is admissible. But \(p^*(G/H)\cong G/J\). Hence \(G/J\) is admissible, i.e. \(J\to G\). \(\square\)

So each summand is \(S^{m|K|/|H|}\) up to finite Weyl-orbit factors. \(\square\)

### Lemma 2 (Cell fixed-point formula)

hence \(H\)-fixed dimension is \([gKg^{-1}\!:\!H]=|K|/|H|\), giving sphere degree \(m|K|/|H|+\epsilon\).

### Lemma 3 (Closure lemma)

Proof: each \(F_H\) is exact and preserves coproducts/filtered colimits. In \(\Sp\), \(\Sp_{\ge r}\) is closed under cofibers, extensions, retracts, coproducts, filtered colimits. ### Lemma 4 (Pure-stratum equivalence)

### Lemma 5 (Module-aisle generation for slice-localizing closure)

\[

### Lemma 9 (Arithmetic optimization)
For \(h>0\), \(c,n\in\mathbb Z\):
\[
c\ge \Big\lfloor \frac nh\Big\rfloor \iff n\le h(c+1)-1,
\]
\[
c\ge \Big\lceil \frac nh\Big\rceil \iff n\le hc.
\]

\[
\sup\{n:\forall H,\ c_H\ge \lfloor n/|H|\rfloor\}
=\min_H\big(|H|(c_H+1)-1\big),
\]
\[
\sup\{n:\forall H,\ c_H\ge \lceil n/|H|\rceil\}
=\min_H |H|c_H.

Proof: first equivalence:
\[
\lfloor n/h\rfloor\le c
\iff n/h<c+1
\iff n<h(c+1)
\iff n\le h(c+1)-1.
\]
Second:
\[
\lceil n/h\rceil\le c
\iff n/h\le c
\iff n\le hc.
\]
Taking simultaneous inequalities over finite admissible \(H\) gives the minima formulas.

If no conjugate of \(H\) lies in \(K\), the result is \(0\). Otherwise \(Y=S^{m\rho_K+\epsilon}\), and
\[
\mathrm{Res}^K_{{}^gH}\rho_K\cong |K:{}^gH|\rho_{{}^gH},
\]
so
\[
\Phi^{{}^gH}(S^{m\rho_K+\epsilon})\simeq S^{m|K:{}^gH|+\epsilon}
= S^{m|K|/|H|+\epsilon}.
\]
Finite indexing set gives a finite wedge. \(\square\)

**Lemma 3 (Closure Lemma).**  
For fixed integer bounds \(r_H\), the class
\[
\{X\in\mathcal C\mid \mathrm{conn}(F_HX)\ge r_H\ \forall H\}
\]
is slice-localizing.

*Proof.* Each \(F_H\) is exact and preserves colimits. In \(\mathrm{Sp}\), \(\mathrm{Sp}_{\ge r_H}\) is closed under coproducts, cofibers/extensions, retracts, and suspension. Therefore \(F_H^{-1}(\mathrm{Sp}_{\ge r_H})\) is slice-localizing. Intersect over finitely many admissible \(H\). \(\square\)

**Lemma 4 (Recollement Lemma).**  
For lower ideal \(U\subseteq P\), there is a triangle
\[
\Gamma_UX\to X\to L_UX
\]
such that for admissible \(K\):
\[
F_K(\Gamma_UX)\simeq \begin{cases}F_KX,& [K]\in U\\ 0,& [K]\notin U,\end{cases}
\quad
F_K(L_UX)\simeq \begin{cases}0,& [K]\in U\\ F_KX,& [K]\notin U.\end{cases}
\]

*Proof.* Existence is Glasman Thm. 2.32. By Cor. 2.33, \(\Gamma_UX\) is supported on \(U\), so \(F_K(\Gamma_UX)=0\) for \([K]\notin U\). Dually \(L_UX\) is supported on \(P\setminus U\), so \(F_K(L_UX)=0\) for \([K]\in U\). Apply \(F_K\) to the triangle to get the displayed identifications. \(\square\)

**Lemma 5 (Pure-Quotient Morita Lemma).**  
For one-step \(U_-\subset U_+=U_-\cup\{[H]\}\),
\[
\mathcal Q_H:=\mathcal C_{U_+}/\mathcal C_{U_-}\simeq \mathrm{Mod}_{R_H},
\quad R_H:=\Sigma^\infty_+W_G(H),
\]
and the distinguished generator \(P_H\) corresponds to \(R_H\).

*Proof.* In \(\mathcal C_{U_+}\), the localization with kernel \(\mathcal C_{U_-}\) identifies the quotient with the subcategory supported on \([H]\) (Lemma 4/Cor. 2.33). That subcategory is \(\mathrm{Mack}(G_H,\mathrm{Sp})\), where \(G_H\simeq BW_G(H)\), hence \(\mathrm{Fun}(BW_G(H),\mathrm{Sp})\). For one-object groupoid \(BW\), this is \(\mathrm{Mod}_{\Sigma^\infty_+W}\). The image of the representable object is the free rank-one module \(R_H\). \(\square\)

**Lemma 6 (Critical Module-Action Lemma).**  
Let \(F_H:=U\Phi^H|_{\mathcal Q_H}\). The right \(R_H\)-module structure on \(F_H(P_H)\) induced by \(\End_{\mathcal Q_H}(P_H)\) is the regular right module.

*Proof.* Under \(\mathcal Q_H\simeq \mathrm{Fun}(BW,\mathrm{Sp})\), \(P_H\) is representable. Hence
\[
F_H(P_H)\simeq \Sigma^\infty_+W,\qquad
\End(P_H)\simeq \Sigma^\infty_+W
\]
(composition ring; equivalently opposite depending on convention). The action \(x\cdot \alpha:=F_H(\alpha)(x)\) is induced by postcomposition on representables, i.e. right multiplication by \(W\). Therefore \(F_H(P_H)\) is the regular right \(R_H\)-module. \(\square\)

**Lemma 7 (Eilenberg–Watts Identification Lemma).**  
\[
F_H|_{\mathcal Q_H}\simeq U_{R_H}:\mathrm{Mod}_{R_H}\to\mathrm{Sp}.
\]

*Proof.* \(F_H\) is exact and colimit-preserving. By Eilenberg–Watts/Morita,
\[
F_H(-)\simeq -\otimes_{R_H} M,\quad M:=F_H(R_H)
\]
as right \(R_H\)-module. By Lemma 6, \(M\cong R_H\) (regular right module), so
\[
F_H(-)\simeq -\otimes_{R_H}R_H\simeq U_{R_H}(-).
\]
\(\square\)

**Lemma 8 (Module-Aisle Generation Lemma).**  
For connective \(R_H\) and \(r\in\mathbb Z\):
\[
\Loc_{\mathrm{sl}}\{\Sigma^mR_H\mid m\ge r\}=(\Mod_{R_H})_{\ge r},
\]
where the right side is the \(t\)-structure created by \(U_{R_H}\).

*Proof.* Each \(\Sigma^mR_H\) (\(m\ge r\)) is \(r\)-connective, and \((\Mod_{R_H})_{\ge r}\) is slice-localizing, so \(\subseteq\). For the reverse inclusion, \((\Mod_{R_H})_{\ge r}\) is generated under colimits and extensions by free modules on \(r\)-connective spectra; and \(\Sp_{\ge r}=\Loc_{\mathrm{sl}}\{\Sigma^mS^0:m\ge r\}\). Applying free \(R_H\)-module functor sends \(\Sigma^mS^0\) to \(\Sigma^mR_H\), giving \(\supseteq\). \(\square\)

**Lemma 9 (Pure Regular Cutoff Lemma).**  
For \(Y\in\mathcal Q_H\):
\[
Y\in\bar\tau_n^{\mathcal O}\cap\mathcal Q_H
\iff
\mathrm{conn}(F_HY)\ge \Big\lceil\frac n{|H|}\Big\rceil.
\]

*Proof.* In \(\mathcal Q_H\), only class \([H]\) remains; regular generators are images of \(G_+\wedge_HS^{m\rho_H}\) with \(m|H|\ge n\). Under \(\mathcal Q_H\simeq\Mod_{R_H}\) and Lemma 7, these are \(\Sigma^mR_H\) with \(m\ge\lceil n/|H|\rceil\). By Lemma 8 this generates \((\Mod_{R_H})_{\ge\lceil n/|H|\rceil}\), i.e. exactly the stated connectivity condition on \(F_HY\). \(\square\)

**Lemma 10 (Pure Slice Cutoff Lemma).**  
For \(Y\in\mathcal Q_H\):
\[
Y\in\tau_n^{\mathcal O}\cap\mathcal Q_H
\iff
\mathrm{conn}(F_HY)\ge \Big\lfloor\frac n{|H|}\Big\rfloor.
\]

*Proof.* Add odd cells \(G_+\wedge_HS^{m\rho_H-1}\), mapping to \(\Sigma^{m-1}R_H\) with constraint \(m|H|-1\ge n\), equivalently \(m-1\ge \lfloor n/|H|\rfloor\). Together with regular cells, the generated shifts are exactly \(\Sigma^jR_H\) for \(j\ge\lfloor n/|H|\rfloor\). Apply Lemma 8. \(\square\)

**Lemma 11 (Isotropy-Induction Lemma).**  
If \(X\) satisfies all fixed-point connectivity bounds (regular or slice versions), then \(X\in\bar\tau_n^{\mathcal O}\) (resp. \(X\in\tau_n^{\mathcal O}\)).

*Proof.* Induct on \(|P|\). If \(|P|=0\), trivial. Choose maximal \([H]\in P\), set \(U_-=P\setminus\{[H]\}\). By Lemma 4:
\[
A:=\Gamma_{U_-}X\to X\to B:=L_{U_-}X.
\]
Then \(A\) has isotropy in \(U_-\), with same \(F_K\)-connectivity bounds as \(X\) for \(K\in U_-\); by induction, \(A\in\bar\tau_n^{\mathcal O}\) (or \(\tau_n^{\mathcal O}\)).  
\(B\) is pure \([H]\)-layer, and \(F_HB\simeq F_HX\), so Lemma 9 (or Lemma 10) gives \(B\in\bar\tau_n^{\mathcal O}\) (or \(\tau_n^{\mathcal O}\)).  
Slice-localizing subcategories are extension-closed, so \(X\) lies in the same filtration piece. \(\square\)

**Lemma 12 (Arithmetic Lemma).**  
For \(d>0\), \(c\in\mathbb Z\cup\{\infty\}\):
\[
c\ge \Big\lceil\frac nd\Big\rceil \iff n\le dc.
\]
Hence
\[
\sup\{n\mid n\le d_i c_i\ \forall i\}=\min_i(d_i c_i),
\]
with \(\infty\)-convention.

*Proof.* For finite \(c\), \(c\ge\lceil n/d\rceil\) iff \(dc\ge n\). For \(c=\infty\), both sides are automatic. The supremum of integers bounded above by all \(d_ic_i\) is their minimum. \(\square\)
