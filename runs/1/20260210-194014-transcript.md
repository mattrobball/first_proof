## Complete Proof

Let \(E = \mathcal D'(\mathbb T^3)\) be the space of distributions on the three-dimensional torus, equipped with its Borel \(\sigma\)-field \(\mathcal B(E)\). Let \(\mu\) be the finite-volume \(\Phi^4_3\) measure on \((E, \mathcal B(E))\).

Fix a nonzero function \(h \in C^\infty(\mathbb T^3)\). We consider the translation map \(T_h: E \to E\) defined by \(T_h(u) = u+h\). Let \(\nu_h\) be the pushforward measure \(T_{h*}\mu\). We wish to show that \(\mu\) and \(\nu_h\) are mutually singular (\(\mu \perp \nu_h\)).

By **Assumption 3** (Hairer's separation property), there exists a measurable set \(A \in \mathcal B(E)\) such that:
\[
\mu(A) = 1 \quad \text{and} \quad \mu(A+h) = 0.
\]
Define the set \(B := A+h\). Since \(T_h\) is a homeomorphism on the topological vector space of distributions (it is a continuous bijection with continuous inverse \(T_{-h}\)), it maps Borel sets to Borel sets. Since \(A \in \mathcal B(E)\), it follows that \(B = T_h(A) \in \mathcal B(E)\).

We now evaluate the measure of \(B\) under \(\mu\) and \(\nu_h\):
1.  **Measure under \(\mu\):** By the construction of \(A\) in Assumption 3, we have:
    \[
    \mu(B) = \mu(A+h) = 0.
    \]
2.  **Measure under \(\nu_h\):** Using **Lemma 1** (Translation-pushforward identity), we have:
    \[
    \nu_h(B) = \mu(B-h).
    \]
    Substituting \(B = A+h\), we get \(B-h = A\). Therefore:
    \[
    \nu_h(B) = \mu(A) = 1.
    \]

We have identified a measurable set \(B\) such that \(\mu(B) = 0\) and \(\nu_h(B) = 1\). By **Lemma 3** (Singularity criterion), this implies that \(\mu\) and \(\nu_h\) are mutually singular:
\[
\mu \perp \nu_h.
\]
Consequently, \(\mu\) and \(\nu_h\) are not equivalent, as equivalent measures must share the same null sets.

## Lemma Proofs

**Lemma 1 (Translation-pushforward identity).**  
For any \(B \in \mathcal B(E)\), \(\nu_h(B) = \mu(B-h)\).

*Proof:*  
By the definition of the pushforward measure \(\nu_h = T_{h*}\mu\), for any measurable set \(B\):
\[
\nu_h(B) := \mu(T_h^{-1}(B)).
\]
The map \(T_h(u) = u+h\) is a bijection with inverse \(T_h^{-1}(v) = v-h\). Therefore, the preimage of the set \(B\) is:
\[
T_h^{-1}(B) = \{u \in E \mid u+h \in B\} = \{v-h \mid v \in B\} = B-h.
\]
Substituting this back into the definition, we obtain:
\[
\nu_h(B) = \mu(B-h).
\]
Q.E.D.

**Lemma 2 (Hairer separating-event lemma).**  
For every \(h \in C^\infty(\mathbb T^3) \setminus \{0\}\), there exists a set \(A \in \mathcal B(E)\) with \(\mu(A)=1\) and \(\mu(A+h)=0\).

*Proof:*

**Lemma 3 (Singularity criterion).**  
If there exists a set \(B \in \mathcal B(E)\) such that \(\mu(B)=0\) and \(\nu(B)=1\), then \(\mu \perp \nu\).

*Proof:*  
Two measures \(\mu\) and \(\nu\) are mutually singular (\(\mu \perp \nu\)) if there exist disjoint sets \(S_\mu, S_\nu \in \mathcal B(E)\) such that \(S_\mu \cup S_\nu = E\), \(\mu(S_\nu) = 0\), and \(\nu(S_\mu) = 0\).
Given a set \(B\) with \(\mu(B)=0\) and \(\nu(B)=1\), we define:
\[
S_\nu = B, \qquad S_\mu = E \setminus B = B^c.
\]
Then:
1.  \(\mu(S_\nu) = \mu(B) = 0\).
2.  \(\nu(S_\mu) = \nu(B^c) = 1 - \nu(B) = 1 - 1 = 0\).
Thus, the partition \(\{B, B^c\}\) witnesses the mutual singularity of \(\mu\) and \(\nu\).
Q.E.D.
